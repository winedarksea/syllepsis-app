Simple and cost effective where possible
Durable, long lasting
Highly insulated, "passive home" design
You want to get as much right as possible on the first try, remodeling is a lot more expensive (digging a slightly bigger hole for foundation isn't that much more expensive, compared to bringing in a crew a second time and having to do it all over while avoiding all the wires and plumbing now added)
These are the biggest expenses in most people's lives. They should be done well and well thought out for desires in advance.
Lots of private spaces
Effective use of space
Spend money on the things you appreciate and on things that will save you money long term
Don't spend money on things you don't appreciate, or will only briefly appreciate
	Function over form. We have a beautiful mosaic on the floor of our entryway matching a beautiful painting at the farm, I don't think anyone has ever noticed them. My mom and I mostly notice them when we water the plants there and spill water on the mosaic floor. Include beauty but don't splash out the cash unless you really think it will add long term value.
Community spaces should be large and open but private spaces don't need to be that big. For those, sound insulation, air quality, and proper lighting matter more.
